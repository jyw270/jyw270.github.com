Game Design UX Best Practices

After reading this article, I started to realize how much detail goes into game design. As a user, I never noticed the placement of specific buttons, icons, and more options, so this article has made me rethink my experiences with games. I agree that when a pop up appears, if there’s an x button, I will automatically close it without reading the content. So, it’s helpful to learn and realize that we should use other methods rather than the x button as a game designer. Some other helpful tips are to consider what is comfortable to reach on the screen, how to arrange user choices for pop-ups, and making prizes significant for watching a rewarded video. I also click the watch video option to get free prizes, sometimes I know what I’m getting and other times it’s randomized, but I agree that if it weren’t a helpful prize, I would not watch the video. I also agree that the rate us dialogs can get pretty annoying when it keeps popping up again and again, so it’s helpful to learn when it is the right time to display it for the user.

Every Picture - Visual Thinking Analysis: Part 1

This is an image of two keychains I assembled as a surprise gift for my friend and I in celebration of our decade long friendship. The keychains are placed side by side, which highlights the meaning behind them – best friends that are close. The shiny metal colors make this image look very bright and lively and it attracts the reader to the image. The letters are our initials and the heart locket contains a photo of us. The sentence on the larger circle shows what these keychains are for and the different colored jewel on each one, represents a hint of the differences in our personalities. This relates to the topic of my collection because I am taking specific keychains that hold a special memory or that I find very unique and cute and presenting what they mean to me and why I value them. The keychains in the image are special to me because I customized them myself and it will be like a souvenir of my 10 years of friendship. My keychain collection is meant to represent memorable happenings in my life and express my personality and likings.

20 Inspirational Examples of Interactive Maps and Street View Experiences in Web Design

I’ve always found the idea of being able to navigate a map in perspective view very intriguing. It’s such a fun experience and the instructions are pretty intuitive. There is consistency with each different map, such as the click and drag method, which makes it very user friendly. I also think it’s very fun to see the map slowly expand and change as you drag to different places. Being able to zoom in and get a closer look to see even more details of interior space is super exciting and satisfies my curiosity. The change in different colors and materials also makes the experience very engaging and keeps the user entertained. April 21, 2021

Best Practices for Modals / Overlays / Dialog Windows

Modals are a really cool feature to implement on a webpage, but it’s not always necessary. Sometimes when modals pop up randomly, I immediately close it without reading it. I agree that this startles the user and that using modals at the right moment is very important. I learned that they can be considered for use when you want to get information from the user, provide additional information not directly related to the parent page, or grab the user’s attention. I also learned that it’s important to provide an escape key for users to exit the modals for convenience. I agree that it is very helpful to match the modal’s title with the button label that the user clicks on because it reminds them what they were working on since the pop up interrupts the user flow. In conclusion, these tips are useful for helping me understand when it is best to use modals.

April 14, 2021

Best Practices For Form Design

As I was reading this article, I couldn't agree more with the key factors mentioned that drive users away from completing a form. When complex questions are asked at the beginning, when there is no page indicator, or when I have to fill out private information, I tend to exit the form. When it comes to personal information, I think it's also important to present credibility on the form to assure them that it is safe to fill out. The best practices for form design seem like obvious considerations a designer would make, but I believe that without reading this article, I would've never thought of some of these tips. So these serve as a reminder for me to consider problems users face and imagine how they would want it to be improved. I find Hollister’s sign in/create account form very user friendly because even though the labels are in the text box, they will appear outside of it when the user clicks on the box. They also have a helpful eye icon to view your password and provide a little note in creating an acceptable password for new members.
