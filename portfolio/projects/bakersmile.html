BakerSmile

My role: Designer

Year: 2021

Tools: Figma, Illustrator, Photoshop

Overview

A non-profit informational website that specializes in designing cakes for underprivileged youth, including youth in foster care, homeless shelters, and low income housing.

Target Audience

  • Middle class young teens and adults ranging from 13-25 years old

  • Have a spacious kitchen workspace and convenient access to ingredients and gadgets

  • Responsible and reliable

  • Confident in their baking and decorating skills

  • Have plenty of free time and enjoy designing cakes to share

User Persona

Comparative Analysis

For Goodness Cakes

  • Cute graphics, cheerful color scheme, and friendly typeface

  • Well designed and organized website

  • Include their contact information: address, email, and phone number

Cake4Kids

  • Less decorative and limited color choices: blue and white

  • Very informative and knowledgeable website

  • Include contact information: address, email, and phone number

Cake4Kids is a credible and informational website, but doesn’t feel welcoming to target users. On the other hand, For Goodness Cakes is a beautifully designed website, but it could use a little more proof of credibility and knowledge like Cake4Kids.

Wireframe

Narrative

  • Main navigation: Home, About, Our impact, Gallery, Chapters, Volunteer, Donate

  • Header: Branding sentence, Finished cake result, Donate and volunteer button

  • Section — Our Mission: About us, Our impact

  • Section — Mini Cake Gallery: 3 x 2 grid: square photos of some cakes made, See more button

  • Section — How You Can Help: Description of volunteering, Description of donating, Links to volunteer, chapters, donate

  • Footer: Contact us, Links to social media

First Prototype

Feedback

  • Site feels a bit text heavy

  • Feels like targeted towards mothers of young children because of the picture of a young girl

  • Square pictures and circle graphics are inconsistent, makes the hierarchy confusing

  • The navigation bar text is incredibly small and seems least important

  • The text for "how you can help" section has too much cognitive load and makes each paragraph seem like its own section

  • Consider making the text hierarchy stronger by using bold text more frequently

  • More color contrast, the white text over them makes things really hard to read

Second Prototype

Feedback

  • Sections need more obvious separation

  • Target audience and call to action are very clear – not overshadowed by the donation buttons

  • Eyes travel directly to that single blue heading at the top of the page

  • The arrows under "What We Do" blend in with the rest of the text

  • If not adding anything else to the footer, consider making it smaller as it calls a lot of attention

  • More differentiation between the “see more” button and “get involved” button to show which one's more important

  • Add more color in the middle sections for consistency

Challenges and Further Development

Challenges

  • Keeping the color scheme and design appropriate, cohesive, and welcoming for target audience

  • Learning to use Figma and applying design principles

Further Development

  • Another section with upcoming events to join or fundraisers

Lessons Learned

  • Consider the purpose and value of feedback before applying to redesigned version

  • No need to start design from scratch; can use inspiration for layout, content, and design
