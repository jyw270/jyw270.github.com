Usability Test Results

User #1: Urvi

When Urvi was working on the tasks, I noticed that she never tried to continue onto the next page without filling in the input and she always clicked on the next button, instead of hiting the enter key. I think she didn't think to hit the enter key because she had to click on the input box already, so she was just used to using the mouse. We are trying to get the input to focus when the form is displayed and I think this way users will be more likely to hit the enter key. Urvi also noticed that the database was printing out all of the responses twice in the gallery when she tried to fill out the form again. This is an error we didn't notice, so we will definitely fix that.

User #2: Penghua

Penghua also never used the enter key to move onto the next page and didn't try to continue without filling out the input. So, getting the input to focus would make people more likley to use that function. I also noticed that the background animation didn't show up on the forms when she tried to fill out the form again from the gallery view. I talked to Ama about this issue and she said that was still a work in progress and that she just didn't bring it back yet.

User #3: Rachel

I noticed that Rachel hit enter once in the beginning and then after that she always clicked on the next button. I asked her if having the input in focus would make her more likely to hit the enter key and she said not necessarily because the next button was pretty eye catching to her. She also suggested that clicking on the title in the gallery view should take you back to the welcome page. I thought this was a great idea and we will probably enable that function. She also suggested that we could add instructions for the mouse interaction we plan to implement on the welcome page to let users know that they can interact with the animation in the background. Letting the users know that available feature is something we forgot to consider, so we will probably add it somewhere in our project.
