Culutural Food Practices

This book provides information on food practices for 15 different cultures. Each chapter talks about a culture's traditional food, dishes, current food practices, special holiday food, and more. Most importantly it discusses diabete risk factors and culturally appropriate counseling recommendations. I think this could be useful for my project because not only does it focus on cultural food practices, but it also discusses health factors, which could help me expand my perspective on how I perceive my topic. Overall, I think the main purpose of this book is to act as a health guide for readers, which I found particularly interesting.

Drago, Lorena, and Cynthia M. Goody. Cultural Food Practices. American Dietetic Association, 2010.

They Eat What? New Year's Food Traditions around the World.

This website shares the different New Year's food traditions from each culture. It provides an image along with a description of the food ingredients and what they represent. This website is useful for my project because it shares a similar idea of what I plan on creating, except I want to allow users to make posts, so that the site is a little more interactive. I feel like the purpose of this reading was to allow viewers to learn about other people's culture and raise awareness about the different New Year's traditions. This is one of my goals for the development of my project, so it is definitely a helpful reference.

Kludt, Amanda. “They Eat What? New Year's Food Traditions around the World.” CNN, Cable News Network, 28 Dec. 2012, https://www.cnn.com/travel/article/new-years-food-traditions/index.html.

Food – Food and Culture

This article talks about how people connect to their culture with food. It discusses how certain cultural backgrounds eat different food and how food can represent a range of things like a culture's values or beliefs, certain practices, and food choices. The author talks about how food plays a role in etiquette, specifically table manners and how it can form different conversations and food habits. I found this article super engaging because it is such a detailed analysis about what food can do. I think this will be helpful for my project because it discusses food from a different perspective. In summary, I think the author's purpose of viewing food from this perspective was successfully delivered.

“Food – Food and Culture.” Food And Culture – Family, People, and Eat – JRank Articles, https://family.jrank.org/pages/639/Food-Food-Culture.html.
